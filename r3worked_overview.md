# R3WORKED — Product Overview

## 0) What R3WORKED is (one-liner)

R3WORKED is a premium website uplift, enquiry-capture, and lead-handling service for underperforming service-business websites — reworking weak digital fronts into sharper, more credible, more conversion-aware commercial assets, with cleaner handling behind the scenes once a prospect takes action.

R3WORKED sits between:
- the existing business and its current online presence
- the prospective customer arriving on the website
- the business owner or team who needs enquiries to land cleanly and be handled properly

It is not just “web design.”
It is not a broad automation consultancy.
It is a focused, repeatable commercial rework system built around three practical layers:

1. Website uplift  
2. Enquiry capture  
3. Lead handling  

---

## 1) What the business actually is

R3WORKED is a narrow, premium service business designed to improve the front-end commercial performance of service companies, initially focused on UK roofers and other service businesses with more value than their current website suggests.

The core thesis is simple:

Many service businesses do not have a traffic problem first.  
They have a trust, clarity, and conversion problem.

Their websites are often:
- visually dated
- poorly structured
- hard to trust
- weak on mobile
- unclear in their call to action
- inconsistent in how enquiries are captured and handled

R3WORKED takes what already exists and reworks it into something commercially sharper.

The service is deliberately constrained so that it can be:
- premium in presentation
- realistic in execution
- repeatable in delivery
- sellable at volume
- maintainable over time

The real offer is not just “make the website look better.”

It is:
- improve the shopfront
- improve the route to enquire
- improve what happens once buying intent lands

## 4) The initial market wedge

## 4.1 Starting point: UK roofers

The first focused target market is UK roofing companies.

This is a strategic decision, not a permanent identity.

Roofers are a good initial wedge because:
- they are local service businesses
- trust matters heavily
- websites are often weak or dated
- the commercial action is simple and valuable: quote request / enquiry
- the before-and-after difference can be made visually obvious
- the service can be made highly repeatable
- English-language sales and positioning are straightforward
- the commercial value of a decent lead is meaningful

The site should therefore feel commercially sharp and premium, while still clearly speaking to a practical service-business audience.

Important:
R3WORKED should not look “roofing branded.”
It should look like a strong modern commercial studio that happens to be starting with roofers.

## 5) The pain R3WORKED removes

## 5.1 Weak first impressions

A large number of service-business websites look as though they were built years ago and then forgotten.

Typical issues:
- dated layouts
- weak typography
- poor spacing
- bad image choices
- generic copy
- cluttered structure
- low trust presentation
- no visual hierarchy
- no obvious commercial path

This creates a silent leak:
people do not always complain — they just do not enquire.

---

## 5.2 Confusing enquiry paths

Even when a visitor wants to get in touch, many websites make the process messy.

Typical problems:
- unclear quote buttons
- too many competing CTAs
- buried contact details
- poor form design
- long or weak forms
- no confidence around what happens after submission
- no distinction between browsing and action-taking

This means real buyer intent gets lost in avoidable friction.

---

## 5.3 Poor lead handling after submission

Many small service businesses do not have a clean process after a lead is submitted.

Typical problems:
- no acknowledgement to the lead
- no instant notification to the business
- no structured record of the enquiry
- inbox chaos
- no visibility on lead flow
- missed or delayed responses
- the website acting as a dead end rather than part of an operating system

R3WORKED improves this without pretending to become a giant CRM implementation.

## 6) What R3WORKED actually does

## 6.1 Core delivery loop

At a high level, the R3WORKED model follows this sequence:

1. Review the current website
2. Identify structural, visual, and conversion weaknesses
3. Rework the front-end experience

5. Implement cleaner form capture

9. Optionally maintain and support the site ongoing

That is the practical system.

## 8) Delivery model

## 8.1 Planned stack

The current intended stack is:

### v1 stack
- Cursor
- Next.js
- Tailwind
- Vercel
- Resend
- Google Sheets

### v2 / workflow layer
- GoHighLevel
- optional WhatsApp / SMS integrations
- lightweight follow-up and pipeline automations where appropriate

This is a practical stack, not a vanity stack.

### Why it works
- modern front-end build quality
- fast deployment
- controlled hosting environment
- coded forms instead of brittle plugin dependence
- straightforward transactional email setup
- simple logging layer that is easy to inspect and explain
- a credible path into smarter follow-through without requiring a giant custom build

---

## 8.2 Hosting and ownership model

The intended operating model is:

- R3WORKED builds and hosts the production website
- the client keeps ownership of their domain
- the client’s domain points into the hosted R3WORKED/Vercel environment
- form handling is managed in code
- email notifications are handled through Resend
- leads are logged into Google Sheets in the initial version
- R3WORKED can charge a monthly support/hosting fee
- future follow-up workflows can sit on top once needed

This model gives:
- speed
- consistency
- control
- repeatability
- lower friction at delivery time

## 14) Footer (credibility layer)

The footer should feel like the final credibility layer of the site: elegant, contactable, policy-backed, and real — without drifting into a generic corporate template.

### Footer structure

#### 14.1 Brand / positioning block

Keep the brand line restrained and high-trust. Example positioning sentence:

“Premium website uplift for service businesses, built around cleaner enquiry capture and smarter follow-up behind the scenes.”

Guidance:
- one concise sentence
- calm, minimal, highly legible
- avoid buzzwords and overclaiming

#### 14.2 Contact block (clearly labeled)

Include a labeled contact area with clear tap targets on mobile.

Structure:
- **Email**: `hello@r3worked.co.uk` (placeholder)
- **Phone**: `+44 (0)20 0000 0000` (placeholder)
- **WhatsApp** (optional): “WhatsApp available for quick questions” or “WhatsApp preferred for first contact” (keep this as a small, quiet line rather than an icon-heavy treatment)

Notes:
- keep it human and direct (no “support desk” tone)
- UK formatting for phone numbers
- include `mailto:` and `tel:` behaviour in the build

#### 14.3 Navigation block (site links)

Include a compact set of links:
- The 3
- Rework
- Pricing
- Review

These should read as intentional sections, not a sitemap dump.

#### 14.4 Legal / trust links

Include policy links even if pages are placeholders initially:
- Privacy Policy
- Terms
- Cookie Policy

Routing can be simple placeholder routes until content exists (e.g. `/privacy`, `/terms`, `/cookies`).

#### 14.5 Bottom strip

Keep the copyright line and add a small, quiet credibility/tagline line if it improves finish.

Example:
- `© {year} R3WORKED. All rights reserved.`
- “Sharper website fronts for serious enquiries”

### Visual direction

- **Layout**: 3–4 calm blocks with generous spacing (avoid dense multi-column grids)
- **Typography**: smaller sizes are fine, but maintain high legibility and clear hierarchy
- **Styling**: minimal dividers, subtle contrast, no icon sets, no generic “footer background slab”
- **Tone**: premium and practical, like a real business you can contact after cold outreach
